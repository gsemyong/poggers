## Candidate Comparison

### Candidate A: Categorized Records

Rejected as a selection candidate. Category boundaries overlap for layout, typography, material,
motion, and generated layers. Fragment merging introduces hidden precedence and multiple ways to state
the same target. Preserving the categories would require more namespaces rather than fewer concepts.

### Candidate C: Target Equations

Retained as a readability control, not a complete language. It is excellent for local scalar review,
but relationships, direct manipulation, presence, layout, generated ownership, and native layers form
a second notation beside the equation table. Complex cases become a property schema plus exceptions.

### Candidate B: Semantic Operations

Current leader. Operations normalize without contribution-order precedence, compose through ordinary
typed recipes, and keep target values, transition policy, layout relations, direct manipulation,
presence, and composition visibly distinct. The stress corpus has not required a backend escape hatch.
Its main cognitive risk is a long flat contribution list and the need to understand target ownership.
Formatting conventions and compiler visualization mitigate this risk, but independent authors must
verify that claim.

## Open Selection Gates

1. Two independent reviewers must classify at least 90 percent of the frozen natural-language corpus
   consistently and record disagreements.
2. At least six eligible TypeScript UI developers must complete the fixed construction,
   modification, diagnosis, theming, and prediction study. No critical or repeated major ownership
   finding may remain.
3. The executable Candidate B Family translation matches the supplied default surface at `361 x 290`
   and the action geometry at `313 x 48`; its key detail and actions now match the production
   reference at `361 x 459.3984375` and `148.5 x 48`. Exact recorded visual comparison at desktop, intermediate, and compact
   sizes, plus one external Figma-authored component, remain required.
4. Complete browser acceptance must cover accessibility-tree output, forced colors, zoom, keyboard
   viewport, real compact touch/drag, multitouch delivery, selection/caret, nested transformed
   composition, actual bundler hot-module replacement, and reduced motion.
5. Production adapter mutation coverage must include fresh-entry ordering, top-layer handoff, StyleX
   ownership, and retained Anime controller disposal.
6. Exhaustive multi-view semantic selection is now integrated with measured layout, preset motion,
   generated layers, focus recovery, and direct drag in the executable Family fixture. The remaining
   gate is exact visual comparison and moving its adapter orchestration from the research fixture into
   the selected production adapter after author review.

## Decision Rule

Candidate B may be selected only if the open gates pass without adding a second way to express any
concern or exposing backend objects. A failed author or browser gate reopens the responsible semantic
law or notation. It does not authorize an escape hatch.

Until then, the correct result is **not yet selectable**. Phase 9 migration remains prohibited.
